The Society in France of the Sons of the American Revolution is the first state society outside the United States of the National Society of the Sons of the American Revolution (NSSAR).

## Purpose of the Society

- To cherish and preserve the memory of those who, during the American Revolution, worked to establish the independence of the American people.
- To foster cordial relations among their descendants.
- To encourage historical research relating to the American War of Independence.
- To maintain the bonds of friendship formed between the French and American peoples at the birth of the American nation.

## Foundation and Early Years

**1889**: The National Society of the Sons of the American Revolution (NSSAR) is founded in
New York. A Frenchman, Edmond, Marquis de La Fayette, is elected Vice President.

**1897**: Creation of a chapter in Paris under the leadership of General Horace Porter, United
States Ambassador to Paris.

**1901**: The law of July 1, 1901 enables associations to develop in France, thereby facilitating
the expansion of the chapter.
Creation of the Society in France

**1926**: The NSSAR authorizes the French chapter to become a full-fledged society.

**1927**: Official creation of the Society in France of the Sons of the American Revolution,
the first non-American society of its kind.

**1934**: Registration as an association under the 1901 law.
Notable Events

**1899–1905**: Search for and transfer of the remains of Captain John Paul Jones to the United
States Naval Academy at Annapolis.

**1910**: The chapter in Paris is chaired by General Horace Porter and has 18 members.

**1917**: Beginning of the annual July 4th ceremonies at the tomb of the Marquis de La Fayette
at the Picpus Cemetery.

**1989**: Erection of a monument at Yorktown in memory of the 75,000 French who took part in
the American War of Independence.

**2010**: Signing of an agreement making the Society in France a partner society of the American
societies with a special semi-autonomous status.

**2013**: The Society in France is recognized as being of public benefit.

### Presidents of the Society

**1927–1988**: Four members of the Pineton de Chambrun family successively serve as president.

**1988–2026**: The Society is successively chaired by Michel de Rochambeau, Hélie de
Noailles, Martin Boyer, and Patrick Mesnard.
