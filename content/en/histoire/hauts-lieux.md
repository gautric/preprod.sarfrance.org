## In Paris

- **[Picpus Cemetery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Picpus_Cemetery#The_Marquis_de_Lafayette)** – Where La Fayette and his wife Adrienne de Noailles are buried. The American flag, renewed every July 4th, has flown over his grave since 1834.
- **[Place des États-Unis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Place_des_%C3%89tats-Unis#Statue_of_Washington_and_Lafayette)** – Monument commemorating the meeting of Washington and La Fayette.
- Read "Paris, Birthplace of the U.S.A." by Daniel Jouve (Gründ editions, 1996), describing 23 Parisian locations that played a significant role in the American Revolution.

## In the Regions

- **[Château de La Grange-Bléneau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau_de_la_Grange-Bl%C3%A9neau)** – Where La Fayette spent the last thirty years of his life.
- **Rochefort** – From where La Fayette departed on the [Hermione](https://fregate-hermione.com/) in 1779. See the [Royal Rope Factory](https://corderie-royale.com/).
- **[Château de Chavaniac](https://www.chateau-lafayette.com/)** – Where La Fayette was born in 1757.
- **Château de Rochambeau** – Where the commander of the French Expeditionary Corps was born and lived. See also his tomb at the [Thoré-la-Rochette](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thor%C3%A9-la-Rochette) cemetery.
- **Saint-Tropez** – Where [Suffren](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Andr%C3%A9_de_Suffren#War_of_American_independence) was born in 1726: statue on the quay.
- **Grasse** – The stronghold of [Admiral de Grasse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Joseph_Paul_de_Grasse) whose intervention was crucial at Yorktown; Tilly (78), where he spent his final days.
- **Toulon** – From where Admiral d'Estaing and the first reinforcements departed. See the [National Naval Museum](https://www.musee-marine.fr/en/our-museums/toulon.html).
- **Brest** – From where Ternay's squadron carrying Rochambeau's Corps departed, as well as the squadrons of de Grasse, Suffren… See the [National Naval Museum](https://www.musee-marine.fr/en/our-museums/brest.html).
- **[Museum of Franco-American Cooperation at Blérancourt](https://museefrancoamericain.fr/en)** – Where American nurses, arriving by the hundreds from 1914, were based.
- **[Port-Vendres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Port-Vendres)** – The obelisk dedicated to the four great works of King Louis XVI: serfdom abolished, freedom of trade, the navy restored, and America made independent.
- **[Versailles](https://www.chateauversailles.fr/)** – Seat of government (King's Council, ministries).
