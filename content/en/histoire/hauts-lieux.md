## In Paris

- **Picpus Cemetery** – Where La Fayette and his wife Adrienne de Noailles are buried. The American flag, renewed every July 4th, has flown over his grave since 1834.
- **Place des États-Unis** – Monument commemorating the meeting of Washington and La Fayette.
- Read "Paris, Birthplace of the U.S.A." by Daniel Jouve (Gründ editions, 1996), describing 23 Parisian locations that played a significant role in the American Revolution.

## In the Regions

- **Château de La Grange-Bléneau** – Where La Fayette spent the last thirty years of his life.
- **Rochefort** – From where La Fayette departed on the Hermione in 1779. See the Royal Rope Factory.
- **Château de Chavaniac** – Where La Fayette was born in 1757.
- **Château de Rochambeau** – Where the commander of the French Expeditionary Corps was born and lived. See also his tomb at the Thoré-la-Rochette cemetery.
- **Saint-Tropez** – Where Suffren was born in 1726: statue on the quay.
- **Grasse** – The stronghold of the admiral whose intervention was crucial at Yorktown; Tilly (78), where he spent his final days.
- **Toulon** – From where Admiral d'Estaing and the first reinforcements departed. See the Naval Museum.
- **Brest** – From where Ternay's squadron carrying Rochambeau's Corps departed, as well as the squadrons of de Grasse, Suffren…
- **Museum of Franco-American Cooperation at Blérancourt** – Where American nurses, arriving by the hundreds from 1914, were based.
- **Port-Vendres** – The obelisk dedicated to the four great works of King Louis XVI: serfdom abolished, freedom of trade, the navy restored, and America made independent.
- **Versailles** – Seat of government (King's Council, ministries).
